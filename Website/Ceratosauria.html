Clade Ceratosauria
=Etrigansauria Delcourt, 2018
Definition - The most inclusive clade containing Ceratosaurus nasicornis but not Passer domesticus.
Diagnosis - enter
Comments - Delcourt (2018) named Etrigansauria as a stem-based clade, defined as "the most inclusive clade including Carnotaurus sastrei & Ceratosaurus nasicornis but not Noasaurus leali". However, this was named based on the analyses of Wang, et al. (2016), which recovered Noasauridae outside of Ceratosauridae+Abelisauridae, a topology which is not as well supported as the traditional Ceratosauridae+(Abelisauridae+Noasauridae) grouping, & under this more standard topology Etrigansauria falls apart. Assuming a Wang, et al. based topology, Etrigansauria is nearly identical to Neoceratosauria (a node-based clade defined as "the least inclusive clade containing Carnotaurus sastrei & Ceratosaurus nasicornis"), wherein the only non-neoceratosaur etrigansaurians are the clade of Berberosaurus+Saltriovenator. As the standard ceratosaur topology is followed on this site, & Berberosaurus & Saltriovenator are the only non-neoceratosaurs present (barring indeterminate taxa that have no bearing on the structure of the tree), Etrigansauria is considered here a junior synonym of Ceratosauria.

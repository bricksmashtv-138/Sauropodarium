Classic "Romerian" anatomical terminology is used on this website. Terminology for laminae & fossae follows Wilson (1999), Wilson, et al. (2011), Wilson (2012), & any subsequently proposed new abbreviations.

Layout & guide to usage

Clade authority
=Synonymous clade authority

Definition - Preferred phylogenetic definition of the clade (where available, official Phylocode definitions are used. If none are available, or no previous ones are sufficient, a new one is informally proposed here, to be formally published at a later date).
Diagnosis - The diagnostic characters found in this clade.
Comments - Any comments in regards to the history, composition, or definition(s) of the clade.

Genus Genus name authority
=synonymous genus authority (if a name is synonymous for any reason besides objective synonymy [e.g. nomina nuda, misspellings], they are specified as such here)

Binomial authority
=synonymous species authority (if a name is synonymous for any reason besides objective synonymy [e.g. nomina nuda, misspellings], they are specified as such here)
The age of the taxon in question, formatted as period, epoch, stage, with further specifics provided where available (e.g. if a taxon is late Maastrichtian as opposed to early)
The formation the taxon is from (or equivalent beds name if there is no formal formation name)
The specific locality data for a taxon, formatted to the lowest possible level, with different quarries/locales given their own entries
Specimens are listed by quarry/locale, with the holotype/paratype/lectotypes/syntypes listed first, then all following specimens listed in alphabetical & numerical order. Entries are formatted as follows: specimen number (if type, specified as such here) (length & mass estimates provided here based on my own rigorous skeletal reconstructions where completed) - List of known elements (length of element in parentheses. If incomplete, one asterisk is used. If using length without condylar ball, two asterisks used)
Diagnosis - Diagnostic characters of taxa (autapomorphies marked with an asterisk; specified if used directly from source, or modified by myself)
Comments - Any comments in regard to the history, taxonomy, diagnosis, locality, scaling rationale, or anything else.

Skeletal or skull reconstructions (or both where applicable) are provided after all entries where I have made sufficiently up to date reconstructions. All skeletals are done in the classic black & white style popularized by Greg Paul, with only preserved elements drawn. Skull reconstructions show missing elements (marked with a dark gray). If a composite reconstruction of multiple specimens, which ones used are specified in the caption, & also which specimens the skeletal is scaled to is specified if necessary. Scale bar value is also provided. All reconstructions are uploaded at their original drawn resolution.

For unnamed taxa, there are listed in alphabetical order after all named taxa of the specified clade. Names are usually provided as nicknames, most commonly based on the locality/formation they were found in, though informally proposed names (e.g. "Nurosaurus") are used where available. Authority on these taxa is somewhat subjective, though generally it is listed as either the first author to mention the specimen(s), or whoever provided the most detailed description of the specimen(s).

Any modifications made to the skeletal/skull reconstructions or the entries themselves are listed on the updates page linked on the homepage, organized by date in descending order.

To prevent overcrowding of pages & repetition, all references are listed in a singular alphabetized bibliography page.

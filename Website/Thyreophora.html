Genus Baiyinosaurus Ning, et al. 2024

Baiyinosaurus baojiensis Ning, et al. 2024
Middle Jurassic, middle Dogger epoch, late Bathonian stage
Straw-Yellow Sandstone Member, Wangjiashan Formation
Pingchuan District, Baiyin City, Gansu Province, China
IVPG-D021 (Holotype) - Partial skull, atlantal intercentrum (23mm), D1, D2 (62mm), five mid-posterior dorsals (78, 69, 78, 84, 81mm), anterior caudal (46mm)
Diagnosis - Frontal wider than long* (1), & not only contributes to the medial margin of the supratemporal fenestra but also makes up a very great contribution to the anterior margin of the supratemporal fenestra* (2); neural arches not greatly elongated dorsally (3); ratio of neural arch height to neural canal height is 1.59 (4); parapophyses are well developed & project somewhat laterally on stalks in anterior view (5); neural spines anteroposteriorly broad & axially expanded in lateral view (6); ratio of neural spine length to centrum length 0.75 (7) (Ning, et al. 2024)

Gobisaurus luoyangensis Xu, et al. 2007
=Zhongyuansaurus luoyangensis Xu, et al. 2007
Middle Cretaceous, middle Gallic epoch, Aptian to Albian stages
Haoling Formation
Shengshuigou, Shaping Village, Liudian Town, Ruyang Basin, Henan Province, China
HGM 41HIII-0002 (Holotype) - Skull, cervical neural spine, dorsals, ribs, caudals, posterior caudal centra, fused distal caudals, humerus, ischia, pubis, osteoderms
Diagnosis - enter
Comments - Xu, et al. (2007) named Zhongyuansaurus luoyangensis for a partial skeleton from Henan Province. Arbour & Currie (2015) noted the material was nearly identical to that of Gobisaurus, barring some diagenetic distortion. This synonymy is followed here, though given the much younger age of "Z." luoyangensis, they are tentatively retained as separate species.
